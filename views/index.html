Commands

There are certain words you can use to let me know what you want me to do. Generally, I give you context-by-context words I expect to get from you. These words are shown in links/buttons that follow some responses I give to you. For instance, from the image below, you can see that I expect you to type either Next or Answer in the given context (I am, however, smart enough to make sense of any other word you type).

Alternatively, you can simply click the links/buttons I present to you if you don't feel like typing.

Starting a conversation

To start a conversation, use basic ice breakers like hi, hello, good day. Or you can just command me to start or begin.

Ending a conversation

To end a conversation, simply tell me bye, later. Or just type words like end, stop, finish.

Asking for a subject

At any time (even while we are working on a question) you can simply type the name of a subject, and we'll switch to that subject. You don't even have to type the full name of the subject.

Going to the next question

If you want to move to the next question just type next.

Yes or no

Most of the other responses I expect from you are yes or no answers.
